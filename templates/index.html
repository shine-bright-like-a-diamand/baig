Drug Development

The process of drug development is very time-consuming and expensive. Currently, there are several types of inhibitors, but they are not able to block any mutations of leukemia. And although scientists are actively working to find a solution, at the moment the problem remains unresolved.

About 1 person in 526 will get CML in their lifetime in the US. And currently there is no medicine that would effectively combat this disease.

Statistics

Chronic myeloid leukemia is a very dangerous disease. About 15% of all leukemia is CML. The American Cancer Society’s estimates for chronic myeloid leukemia in the US for 2022 are:
- About 8,860 new cases will be diagnosed with CML

- About 1,220 people will die of CML

BCR-ABL

BCR-ABL is a hybrid protein, a product of the BCR-ABL1 hybrid gene, formed as a result of the erroneous attachment of a part of chromosome 9 (ABL gene) to chromosome 22 (BCR gene). The result of this abnormal process is a new gene that causes the synthesis of a new protein that leads to the formation of malignant blood cells in the bone marrow.

Currently, there are three groups of major BcrAbl TC inhibitors: inhibitors of the first (imatinib), second (nilotinib, dasatinib) and third (ponatinib) generations. The first selective inhibitor of Bcr-Abl TC was imatinib..
